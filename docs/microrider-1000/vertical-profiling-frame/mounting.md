# Mount the Instrument

Mount the MicroRider-1000, external battery and frame guards onto the vertical profiling frame.

## Procedure

### 1. Mount the MicroRider

!!! important

    Two people are recommended when mounting the MicroRider. One person should support the instrument while the second secures the mounting hardware.

<photo>

1. Lay the frame on its side.
2. Loosen the MicroRider mounting brackets using a **13 mm spanner**.
3. Place the MicroRider into the mounting brackets.
4. Ensure the probe section protrudes below the bottom of the frame, as shown below.
5. Tighten the mounting hardware securely.

!!! important "Instrument position"

    The MicroRider must protrude below the bottom of the frame to ensure the microstructure probes sample undisturbed water during profiling.

---

### 2. Mount the External Battery

<photo>

1. Loosen the external battery mounting brackets using a **10 mm spanner**.
2. Position the external battery with the bulkhead connector facing the same direction as the MicroRider.
3. Tighten the mounting hardware securely.

---

### 3. Connect the Battery Y-Cable

<photo>

1. Remove the **8-pin** and **6-pin** dummy connectors from the MicroRider and external battery, respectively.
3. Clean and apply a light coating of **DC4 silicone grease** to the connectors.
4. Match the labels on the battery Y-cable to the corresponding connectors.
5. Connect the battery Y-cable to the MicroRider and external battery.
6. Purge trapped air from each connector before fully tightening the locking sleeves.
7. Leave the communication connector accessible for instrument configuration.

!!! warning

    Never force a connector. If resistance is encountered, stop check the pin alignment before reconnecting.

---

### 4. Install the Frame Guards

<photo>

1. If the frame guards are not already installed, mount them to the bottom of the frame.
2. Align the mounting holes with the lower frame members.
3. Use **10 mm spanner** a to secure the guards using the supplied fasteners.
4. Confirm the Microrider is set back far enough to protect the MicroRider probes.

!!! important

    The frame guards protect the probes during handling and recovery. They must not contact the probes during normal operation.
